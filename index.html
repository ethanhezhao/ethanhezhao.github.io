Biography

I’m a research fellow in Monash University working with Dinh Phung.

I’m interested in statistical machine learning and deep learning, including Bayesian modelling and inference, optimal transport, robustness of deep learning, as well as their applications in computer vision, natural language processing, graph analysis, recommender systems, and etc.

I obtained my PhD, Master, and Bachelor degrees from Monash University (2019), Nanjing University, and Nankai University, respectively.

Projects and Software

Please also see my software on the Monash Machine Learning Group website.

MetaTM

Topic Modelling with Metadata
Last update: Jul 2020
MetaTM consists of the software packages of a series of the state-of-the-art topic models for text analysis, which leverage metadata such as document labels and word embeddings to boost the performance and interpretability of topic modelling. In particular, MetaTM enjoys significantly better performance for analysing short texts, such as tweets or news abstracts. MetaTM can be used to visualise the topical content of a given corpus, as well as to performs tasks like document classification and clustering. MetaTM consists of three packages: i) MetaLDA (ICDM 2017 paper, code) incorporates binary metadata, with a scalable multi-thread Java implementation; ii) MetaFTM (ACML 2017 paper, code) enables topics to focused on the most related words informed by word embeddings; iii) MIGA (ACL 2019 paper, code) is a topic-based document clustering model informed by document labels.

NBVAE

Negative-Binomial Variational AutoEncoder
Last update: Mar 2020
NBVAE (AISTATS 2020 paper) is the software of a state-of-the-art Variational AutoEncoder (VAE) for modelling discrete data such as texts or relational data. NBVAE achieves improved performance on multiple tasks including text analysis, collaborative filtering, and multi-label classification. NBVAE is implemented in TensorFlow and runs efficiently with GPUs.

TSL

Topic Structure Learning
Last update: Dec 2018
TSL consists of the software packages of a series of the state-of-the-art topic models for text analysis, which discovers topics with informative structures from documents in an unsupervised way. TSL enjoys significantly better performance and interpretability for analysing both long and short documents. MetaTM can be used to visualise the topical structures of a given corpus, as well as to perform tasks like document classification and clustering. TSL consists of two packages: i) DirBN (NeurIPS 2018 paper, code) automatically discovers tree-structured topic hierarchies, where topics on the higher levels are more general than those on the lower levels. ii) WEDTM (ICML 2018 paper, code) discovers sub-topics for each normal topic, which give finer-grained semantic interpretations of both topics and documents.

BMLS

Bayesian Multi-label Learning with Sparse Features and Labels
Last update: May 2018
BMLS (AISTATS 2018 paper) is the software of a state-of-the-art Bayesian multi-label classification model with improved performance and efficiency by leveraging the sparsity in both features and labels. In addition, by leveraging label correlations, BMLS works effectively in the case where the labels of a datum are partially missing.

NARM

Node Attribute Relational Model
Last update: Aug 2017
NARM (ICML 2017 paper) is the software of a state-of-the-art Bayesian random graph model that incorporates the node metadata (attributes) in a relational graph. NARM can be used for modelling different kinds of relational graph such as social networks, bibliographic networks, and drug interactions, in the tasks of link prediction and community detection.
